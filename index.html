Your Roadmaop to Modern Intimacy, inspired by 5 tips on how to touch a woman

The landscape of intimacy has shifted. What worked for previous generations no longer serves the relationships we're building today. Women want to love men again, but on new terms that prioritize safety, presence, and genuine connection alongside performance and technique.

Your Nervous System Regulates Hers

Before your words matter, before your touch lands, her nervous system is reading yours. Safety is the foundation for desire. Learning to regulate your own nervous system, to bring intentional slowness and grounded presence, changes everything. Pacing matters more than technique. Your ability to stay present, to not rush, signals safety in ways that words never can.

Presence Without Agenda

Build the foundation for intimacy. Eye contact, hand-holding, being fully present without rushing toward an outcome, this is where intimacy actually builds. Touch without expectation unlocks everything. When you can be with her without needing it to go somewhere, genuine connection becomes possible.

Building Trust Through Touch

Not all touch is equal. Earning access to intimate zones starts with respecting neutral territory first. Start neutral, build toward intimate. Even in long-term relationships, never assume. Read her regulation signals constantly. The spheres of touch: from shoulders and back to more intimate areas, this creates a pathway of trust that bypasses performance and lands in genuine response.

How You Touch Matters

Master the three qualities of touch: pressure, speed, and rhythm. Vary them intentionally. Slow touch builds tension and anticipation. Be unpredictable in delightful ways. The quality of your touch, how present you are in each moment, matters as much as where you touch. This is about responding to what's actually happening in real time.

Receiving Touch: The Missing Skill

Most men have never learned to receive. You've learned to perform, to give, to earn. But receiving without reciprocating immediately is a skill. It will deepen intimacy for both of you and drive her wild. Just wait util she finds out you can be touched without rushing into action, when you can simply receive pleasure and model vulnerability that makes genuine connection possible.

Who is ANANNA?

ANANNA is a collective voice for women who believe men deserve better tools for intimacy and body awareness. Our work is grounded in somatic practice, nervous system science, and the lived experience of women who want genuine connection just as much as you do.

We created this space because the skill gap between what women experience and what men understand about intimacy can be learned. Not through performance or technique, but through presence, safety, and the willingness to feel.

This is not therapy. This is practical, body-based guidance for men who are ready to show up differently.

Essential Practices

Practice 01

Self-Regulation Check-In

5 minutes before intimacy

Your nervous system sets the tone for hers. Learn to check your own regulation state before any intimate moment. Download our free somatic audio for additional guidance.

1
Before any intimate moment, pause
2
Close your eyes and scan your body
3
Notice: Where am I tense? How's my breath?
4
Ask: Am I actually regulated or rushing?
5
Rate yourself: Calm (1-10) or Anxious?
6
Decide: Do I need to regulate first or am I ready?
Practice 02

Presence Check

During and after intimacy

Learn to recognize the difference between genuine presence and performed technique in the moment.

Was I in my head or in my body?
Did I respond to her or follow a script?
Was there spontaneity or predictability?
Did my nervous system stay regulated?
Was I present or performing?
One word: Connected or Distant?
Practice 03

Receiving Touch

Ongoing practice

Most men never learned to receive. Practice being touched without immediately reciprocating.

1
Ask your partner to touch you (back, shoulders, scalp)
2
Your only job: Receive. Don't reciprocate yet.
3
Notice the urge to "give back" immediately
4
Stay with receiving for 5-10 minutes
5
Breathe. Let yourself be cared for.
6
After: Notice how it felt to just receive
